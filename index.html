ABOUT THE TEMPLE

The Baba Dhabaleswar Shiv Temple of Daradipal is a sacred shrine that radiates peace, devotion, and cultural heritage. Surrounded by the beauty of nature, it is the spiritual heart of the village.

To the south of the temple flows the holy Kushei River, whose gentle waters bring purity and prosperity to the land. On the north side lies a serene pond, where the calm reflections of the temple add to its divine charm. Inside the premises, tall coconut trees stand gracefully, making the temple environment refreshing and sacred.

Every day, devotees gather here to offer prayers to Lord Shiva. The sound of conch shells, temple bells, and chanting creates an atmosphere of divinity. During Maha Shivratri, the temple comes alive with thousands of devotees from nearby villages. All-night prayers, bhajans, and offerings of bilva leaves, flowers, and coconuts make the celebration truly divine. The Baba Dhabaleswar Temple is not only a place of worship but also a symbol of unity, tradition, and faith for the people of Daradipal. Blessed by the presence of the Kushei River and embraced by nature, it continues to inspire devotion and harmony in the hearts of all who visit.
